This course introduces students to theories and practical examples involving social science, socio-economic change, and environmental resources, especially between developed and developing countries. There are tangible resources and intangible resources, as will be shown in examples of ways that differing resources sustain personal, societal, and international communities. Tangible resources include food, clothing and shelter. Intangible resources include education, knowledge, personal abilities, social capital, and political power relations. We look at these within interpersonal relations, gender relations, socio-economic relations, and international relations.

発展途上地域と先進地域の間の資源探しや社会経済的な変化を紹介する。理論と実践双方ともについて、解釈や動因するスキルを身に付ける方法を勉強する授業である。 現代社会の中には、生活を可能にする資源をお互いに探さなければならない。こうした資源は無形のもでもあり、有形のものでもある。自己においての社会位置を支える資源または社会の国際舞台での位置を支える資源がその例となる。有形のものは衣食住などの資産となるが、無形の資源は、教育、知識、個人能力、ソシアルキャピタル、政治的な力関係、などと考えられる。必修文献の中のこれらの例は、個人間、ジェンダー間、階層間、国家間まで渡る例である。

Design maintaining nature capital and conserving ecosystem service is necessary for sustainable development in both urban and rural area, also natural reserve. The design with nature using ecosystem services wisely is called as ecological design. In the class students study concepts of ecological design, spacial planning, natural and living materials for design, nature restoration, and monitoring methods through a whole process of project. Students should understand natural processes, and basic concepts and methods of ecological design in the class. There will be lectures including guest ones, fieldworks and group works. Also, there should be some reports during and after a class.

エコロジカルなデザインとしての人と自然の調和をめざした計画、そしてデザインでは、都市から農村、さらには自然が残る海岸や山岳地帯まで様々な場面において、自然科学に支えられた技術として、きめ細かい配慮をふまえた計画プロセスとして、実行性のある提案が求められている。講義では、生態系を保全し、生物多様性を維持するためのエコロジカルデザインについて、空間計画とデザインの考え方から、自然素材、生物素材への理解、管理とモニタリングによる再生などの一連のプロセスを事例を通して紹介する。この講義の目的は、自然についての理解をすすめ、これからのエコロジカルデザインのありかたを考える基礎とするものである。

Energy is essential infrastructure of economic and social activity in modern society. It sometimes works as an incentive, and sometimes as restricting factor for economic and social activity. Considering energy self-sufficiency, Japan is limited fossil fuel resources, and we have to fulfill both energy security and contribution for global warming as a developed country. What kind of energy resorces should be selected for the sake of mid and long-term sustainable economy? Basically no energy resources are faultless at the point of energy security, environment adaptability, economic efficiency and public acceptance. We learn further energy-environmental policy and technological innovation in order to seek energy best mix.

エネルギーは、近代社会において経済社会活動の重要なインフラストラクチャであり、インセンティブである一方、その制約要因でもあります。化石燃料資源に乏しい日本においては、エネルギー安全保障に留意しながら、先進国として気候変動問題へ貢献を果たさなければなりません。では、われわれは持続可能な経済社会のために、中長期的にどのようなエネルギー資源を選択すべきでしょうか。供給安定性、環境適合性、経済効率性、社会受容性の面で万能なエネルギー資源はありません。ベストミックスを探求するための方法論として、政策と技術革新の現状と展望について学びます。

Natural environment as a composition of ecosystems provides a variety of ecosystem services to human beings. however, Its capacity to accommodate human need is limited. When the pressure exceed the capacity, ecosystems may change irreversibly and enter to a unstable and unsustainable state. This sounds self-evident to every one but on paper only, no real experience. This course interpret high resolution images and calculate the land price, agricultural products, and CO2 absorptions through simple exercise. Student will be provided a variety of practices of visual Identity design by invited Professor Kashiwa Sato, who is a leading Art Director/Creative Director in Japan.

Theme: Designing tenacious healthy society Nowadays, patients are expected to participate in their own medical care program actively to realize Active Healthcare Society. In this year’s class, we will propose a new concept/standard of Home Healthcare. Class will be formated in groups to develop the ideas through discussion and prototyping. Students can learn the knowhow of effective strategic design in workshop style. They can also get feedbacks from Professors through presentations in each stage.

今期のテーマ：火星居住

極限環境での居住を可能にする建築やプロダクトの設計,デザインを考えます。 極寒地,豪雪地帯,災害地,洋上,海底,宇宙など,困難な条件が多く存在する環境で,積極的に開拓し居住しようとする人類。その冒険的な活動を,我々クリエイターからの視点で学び,創作していきます。 重量,素材,運搬,活動目的,発展性,汎用性,社会性,エネルギー問題,気候条件,など客観的かつ総合的な解決が求められます。好奇心旺盛な学生と共に,冒険的な設計を楽しみたいと思っています。

Theme: Mars Habitation

Designing Architecture/Product for extreme environment is our mission. We, human-being, explore aggressively towards to difficult environment, such as cold climate, heavy snowfall, dry land, ocean, post-disaster emergency site, outer space, etc. We are going to experiment the adventurous project with creative mind. There are many factors to be concerned, weight, material, structure, transportation, purpose, possibility, versatility, social and energy solution. It requires objective and comprehensive point of view. I would like to collaborate with the students who have a lot of curiosity and motivation.
